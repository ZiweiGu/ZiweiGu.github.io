I am a data scientist at Lyft, where I work on user segmentation and experiment design. I graduated with a bachelors in Mathematics and Computer Science (December 2020) and masters in Computer Science (May 2021) from Cornell University.

I have been working with Professor Jeffrey Rzeszotarski since Spring 2019 and was previously advised by Professor Claire Cardie and Professor Madeleine Udell at Cornell.

My current research focuses on developing novel interactive systems to (1) help people make sense of data and AI fairness issues and (2) support human-centered AI. I'm broadly interested in human-computer interaction, social computing, machine learning, and algorithm fairness.

I am actively seeking PhD advisors for Fall 2022.

paper / code / slides

We trained a deep learning model (Transformer) for open information extraction, modeled as a sequence to sequence transduction task. We showed that our model was competitive with state-of-the-art systems, but without the dependencies on other NLP tools.
